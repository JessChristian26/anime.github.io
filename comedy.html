The Best Anime of Every Genre

Looking for the best anime per genre? This is your guide to the best anime shows in almost every genre? These popular shows defined
their genre to the point where their influence radiates out throughout all of anime today.

Action, Comedy - October 28, 2022

The Most Popular Anime: Comedy

Facebook Pinterest Linkedin Twitter

Humor is almost always part of the storytelling in anime. While it is often overshadowed by intense fights,
poignant drama, and colorful romance, almost every anime has a comedic moment. Like fantasy and shone
anime, comedy anime span a wide range of styles and stories. No two comedy anime are going to be exactly
alike and each series brings something new for the viewers.

My Hero Academia: 8.5/10

With a show as bright and colorful as My Hero Academia there is no way anyone jumped into it without
thinking there were going to be some laugh out loud moments in store. If they did they were dead wrong
because this is definitely one of the funniest anime of the year.

With so many different characters, the humor in this one takes many different forms, which is a plus because
if one joke falls flat another will be sure to tickle your fancy. Trust us when we say, you'll be laughing as early
as episode one.

Haikyuu!: 8.6/10

Remember when we said sports anime and comedy go together like peanut butter and jelly? That goes
double for Haikyuu!!, as it is easily one of the best series of the year when it comes to humor. We really have
to give the spotlight to the characters RyÅ«nosuke Tanaka and Nishinoya Yuu, one of the best comedy duos
introduced in this decade of anime.

That is no exaggeration by any means. Those that have watched Haikyuu!! know exactly what we mean,
and to those that decide to jump in, you will learn.

Shin Chan: 8.6/10

Shin Chan recently received a new English dub and with a new English dub came a new 2015 rerelease of
everyone's favorite anime kid that talks a little too old for his age. What's there to say about the comedy of
Shin Chan that hasn't already been said. It's a series as old as time and is one of the most well-known
comedy anime out there.

Seriously, while it can be immature, Shin Chan has some of the funniest jokes in all of anime. If you want
something a bit more relatable, however, we recommend the more U.S. localized Funimation dub.

Hajime No Ippo-Rising: 8.7/10

Remember what we said about humor and sports anime with our past two sports entries? We want you to
take that and multiply it by infinity because the entire Hajime no Ippo series very well may be the most
hilarious experience you will have with a sports anime.

This series has zero jokes that fall flat and just about all of them are timed and executed to perfection.
Basically every character has a golden moment that will have you rolling on the floor, take our word for it.

One Punch Man: 9.8/10

There is no doubt that this is one of the best comedy anime of the decade. One Punch Man's basic premise
is a hero that can defeat any enemy with a single punch. That is funny enough on its own but that's not all
there is to the humor, not with this diverse cast.

Of course, there's the title character Saitama who just wishes he could fight someone with winning after one
hit and can carry casual conversations during battle. You've got Genos, the cyborg sidekick that keeps
pushing himself to get stronger but gets dismembered literally every time he fights. The funniest character,
however, would definitely have to be King who we really don't want to spoil anything about. Just please give
this one a chance if you want a good couple of laughs.

The Best Anime of Every Genre

Looking for the best anime per genre? This is your guide to the best anime shows in almost every genre? These popular shows defined
their genre to the point where their influence radiates out throughout all of anime today.

The 5 Most Popular Anime: Slice of Life

Facebook Pinterest Linkedin Twitter

Different from most shonen anime which contain intense fights or fast-paced action, slice-of-life anime
feature the more mundane moments of everyday life. Unlike fantasy or sci-fi series, slice of life anime feel
much more relatable since the characters experience the same things as the audience. Slice-of-life anime
often coincides with the comedy and romance genre of anime.

Violet Evergarden: 8.4/10

Violet Evergarden is a gorgeously animated show, with a quality that rivals some of the best anime out
there. But there's more to this show than just superficial beauty. The plot takes place in the aftermath of a
bloody war, one in which our protagonist Violet played a violent role. After being seriously injured in a fight,
she starts a new life working at a postal services company, where she becomes an Auto Memory
Doll, transcribing people's thoughts and feelings onto paper. Along the way, viewers are treated to a journey
of beautiful self-discovery.

March Comes In Like A Lion: 8.4/10

Few shows better capture the bleakness of depression than March Comes In Like A Lion. The main character
Rei Kiriyama is a reclusive and isolated person who avoids seeing others. Despite being a professional shogi
player, Rei lives a listless life - one that is shaken up by the Kawamoto Sisters when they welcome him into
their warm family environment. This show's slow pace and careful character study make for an enthralling
slice of life that leans more on the drama than the comedy. All of this is supplemented by a distinctive art
style that draws viewers in.

Mob Psycho 100: 8.5/10

This is a show that celebrates the normal despite our main protagonist being anything but. Mob Psycho 100
features Shigeo "Mob" Kageyama, a powerful psychic who chooses not to use his powers unless necessary.
Working with the con artist Arataka Reigen, he exorcises evil spirits.

Though these ingredients seem to be the formula for a dramatic show, there is plenty of time devoted to
ordinary life and everyday pursuits. There is also a lot more fighting than in your typical slice of life show.
Together it creates a unique atmosphere of lighthearted comedy and intense action.

Beastars: 8.5/10

Beastars takes a lot of inspiration from Disney hit Zootopia. Both take place in a modern world where
animals co-exist uneasily, divided up between herbivores and carnivores. But where the animated film
focused on a police department, Beastars features a school drama club. After the death of his alpaca friend,
grey wolf Legoshi meets a small rabbit called Haru for whom he begins to develop complicated feelings.
This show deals heavily with prejudice and human (animal) nature while also creating an interesting world
that viewers will find themselves drawn into.

Mushishi Zoku Shou: 8.9/10

An episodic show that follows the life of Mushishi Ginko, Mushishi Zoku Shou is a continuation of a classic
that aired in the mid-2000s. Ginko travels throughout the fantasy landscape, helping normal humans adapt
and live with magical beings called Mushi. The show's segmented nature works to its advantage as
each episode tells a satisfying story while giving us a glimpse into the lives of its characters.

This is all backed up with a surreal, beautifully-drawn world. This show may explore the mystery of its nature,
but it's no mystery why this anime is so beloved.

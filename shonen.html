The Best Anime of Every Genre

Looking for the best anime per genre? This is your guide to the best anime shows in almost every genre? These popular shows defined
their genre to the point where their influence radiates out throughout all of anime today.

Action, Shounen - October, 28 2022

The 5 Most Popular Anime: Shounen

Facebook Pinterest Linkedin Twitter

Shonen anime is targeted at a demographic of boys and young men. Perhaps because of this demographic,
these genre of anime often feature male protagonists. However, shonen anime covers many different genres,
from romance, action, slice-of-life, fantasy and more.Shonen anime are also famous for high-action series
containing impressive fights with themes of camaraderie and hard work.

Disastrous Life of Saiki: 8.3/10

Saiki Kusuo is a boy with psychic powers. These powers consist of telepathy and x-ray vision, but he finds
them as an inconvenience. Just wanting to live a normal life, Saiki lives each day trying to keep his powers a
secret. Because of his powers, Saiki ends up meeting Riki Nendou and Shun Kaidou and saying goodbye to
his dreams of a normal life.

The Disastrous Life of Saiki K. was released during the summer 2016 season and was adapted by studios
J.C Staff and Egg Firm. The anime is a short running for five minutes per episode. It aired for 120 episodes
and has a second season.

Dragon Ball Super: 8.4/10

Dragon Ball Super takes place seven years after the events of Dragon Ball Z. There is a new threat to earth in
the form of Beerus, the God of Destruction. A prophecy states that Beerus will be defeated by a Super Saiyan
God, he is determined to prove this fate untrue. Together with his assistant Whis, Beerus searches for said
Saiyan.

Dragon Ball Super was released during the summer 2015 season and was adapted by Toei Animation studio.
The anime is 131 episodes and was the most recent season in the Dragon Ball series.

JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure: 8.4/10

The JoJo’s series is one of the most popular continuously airing seasons of the modern age. The series takes
place in 1868 where Dario Brando saves the life of George Joestar. To repay Dario, George takes in his son
Dio to repay his debt. Unsatisfied with the events, Dio aspires to destroy George and his son Jonathan
Joestar, known as JoJo. This would create a legacy of JoJo’s descendants combating Dio and the Pillar Men’s
antics.

JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure is 26 episodes and was adapted by studio David Production. The anime is
26 episodes and the series is split into parts.

My Hero Academia: 8.5/10

Alongside JoJo’s as a popular airing anime is My Hero Academia. In a world where people are born with
quirks (Superpowers), Deku is born without one. However, this doesn’t stop him from wanting to become a
hero. Deku trains his body to become like his idol, the number one hero All Might.

After meeting All Might, Deku is deemed worthy of being passed down All Might’s quirk, One For All. Deku
aims to become the number one hero after All Might’s time is done. My Hero Academia was released during
the spring 2016 season and was adapted by studio Bones. The anime is 13 episodes and is airing its fourth
season during the current winter 2020 season.

Hunter x Hunter (2011): 8.9/10

Rounding out the list as the greatest Shonen anime of the decade, Hunter x Hunter takes the top spot.
As one of the best remakes in anime, Hunter x Hunter follows Gon Freecss. Gon takes the hunter’s exam to
become a full-fledged Hunter like his father. Gon’s father abandoned him at a young age and Gon wants
to find him. The road is tough for a 12-year-old so Gon adventures with his friends Leorio Paladiknight,
Killua Zoldyck, and Kurapika.

Hunter x Hunter was originally released during the fall 1999 season before its fall 2011 remake and is one of
studio Madhouse’s best series. The anime is 148 episodes and aired for one season.
